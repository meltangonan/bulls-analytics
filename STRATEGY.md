# @chicagobullsdata Strategy

## Target problem

An everyday Bulls fan who wants league-quality data storytelling *specifically
about the Bulls* has nowhere to go. The high-craft NBA viz accounts (Basketball
University, Kirk Goldsberry, Owen Phillips) are all league-wide; the
Bulls-specific accounts are fan/meme/occasional-stat pages with no craft. That
single-team-plus-craft intersection is empty for *every* team — not because it
was tried and failed, but because nobody with the craft has bothered.

## Our approach

Win on craft: every Bulls post should look like it belongs next to the best NBA
viz accounts — Basketball University, Kirk Goldsberry, Owen Phillips — but the bar
is *pretty **and** instantly legible to a normal Bulls fan*, not just pretty. That
combination rules out both the ugly stat-dumps the fan pages post and the
beautiful-but-inscrutable charts only stats people decode, so the account owns the
empty single-team-craft corner by being eye candy a casual fan actually gets.

## Who it's for

**Primary:** The everyday Bulls fan who'd never call themselves a stats person —
they're hiring the account to show them a Bulls stat they didn't know, as a chart
clean enough to get in two seconds and pretty enough to stop the scroll ("huh,
didn't know that").

**Secondary (a bonus, not the design target):** Data-literate hoops nerds and the
"top guys" in the space, whose reposts and respect are gravy. Decision rule: when
a detail would help a nerd but confuse a casual fan, the casual fan wins.

## Key metrics

- **Shares / sends per post** — someone sent your chart to a friend or dropped it
  in a group chat; the belonging signal, chosen over saves on purpose (sharing is
  social, saving is solitary). *Instagram Insights, weekly.*
- **Engagement rate per post** — likes + saves + shares + comments relative to
  reach; do the followers you *have* actually care? A rate, so it can regress even
  as followers grow. *Instagram Insights, weekly.*
- **Community recognition** — reposts, collabs, shoutouts, and follows from other
  Bulls pages and the "top guys." The metric closest to the actual goal.
  *Qualitative running note, moves over months.*

## Tracks

### Craft & the format library

The reusable visual grammar (boards, tables, grids, shared-scale comparisons, the
occasional court map) plus the polish that clears the top-tier bar (Basketball
University / Goldsberry / Owen Phillips). Your *visual voice* lives here for now.

_Why it serves the approach:_ the craft bet **is** this track — it's where "pretty
+ instantly legible" gets built and standardized so every post clears the bar.

### The idea-to-post engine

The steady supply of genuinely surprising Bulls angles — idea catalog,
clarification gate, turning Bulls data into "didn't know that" — posted *often*.
Your *editorial POV* (which stats you find worth charting) lives here.

_Why it serves the approach:_ craft needs substance to apply it to; this
manufactures the surprise, and its tempo — post regularly and often — is itself a
top growth lever (per Owen Phillips, consistency is the key to success).

### Community & distribution

Showing up in the Bulls-fan community: collabs and cross-posts with other Bulls
pages, engaging where the persona already hangs out, earning reposts. Your "top
guy for data" *reputation* is built here.

_Why it serves the approach:_ belonging is the goal and the #1 no-budget growth
lever — a niche account grows by *borrowing* other pages' audiences, not by
outspending anyone.

## Not working on

- Chasing raw follower count as the headline goal — belonging in the community
  beats scale.
- League-wide coverage — single-team focus is the whole point; breadth dilutes
  the corner.
- Fan-page content (memes, stories, hot takes) — that lane is taken, and it isn't
  the craft bet.
- Designing charts for the data nerds first — see the persona decision rule.

## Marketing

**One-liner:** The top account in the space — but for data. *Chicago Bulls,
charted.*

**Key message:** Basketball-University-quality data viz pointed entirely at the
Bulls, made legible and fun for any fan — not just stats people.
