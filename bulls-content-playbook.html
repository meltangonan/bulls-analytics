North star and creative system

The Bulls visual encyclopedia

Build a visual encyclopedia of Bulls basketball—current, historical, comparative, and understandable.

The account does not need one permanent subject or a rigid set of series. Each post can be discovered by combining four choices. Peak season, full Bulls tenure, and peak-versus-longevity are not competing definitions; they are three valid posts generated from the same system.

1 · SubjectWhat is being studied?

Player, lineup, team, era, salary, draft, trade, zone, or playing style.

2 · LensWhat is the question?

Peak, longevity, value, rarity, change, fit, rank, similarity, or historical company.

3 · TimeframeWhat is the comparison window?

Game, stretch, season, Bulls tenure, decade, era, or franchise history.

4 · GrammarWhat makes it obvious?

Leaderboard, table, grid, quadrant, timeline, court, or shared-scale comparison.

Selection rule: start with an interesting basketball question and comparison group. Let the question choose the timeframe and visual. A format is a tool, not a content strategy.

Prompts, not commitments

Guided Idea Bank

These are generative lanes rather than a ranked roadmap. Any one could produce a single post, a short run, or a recurring format if the data and audience response justify it.

Player Comparisons

Player historyMultiple timeframesBoards · quadrants · grids

Compare Bulls players through several legitimate frames instead of forcing one all-time formula. A single season reveals peak; an entire Bulls tenure reveals sustained value; showing both reveals the tension between them.

Prompt starters
  • Greatest individual Bulls seasons
  • Best full Bulls tenures
  • Peak versus longevity
  • Players at the same age or career stage
  • Most similar Bulls across different eras
  • How a player's role changed during his Bulls career

Useful lenses: impact, efficiency, usage, skill mix, durability, playoff translation, and historical company.

Team & Era Comparisons

Franchise historyShared fingerprintsTables · profiles · timelines

Describe Bulls eras with the same small family of measures so their identities become comparable. The goal is not to declare every older team better or worse; it is to show how each team tried to win.

Prompt starters
  • What made each Bulls era distinct?
  • First three-peat versus second three-peat
  • Rose-era offense versus Jordan-era offense
  • Most balanced Bulls teams
  • Which historical Bulls team resembles the current roster?
  • How roster age, scoring concentration, pace, and shooting changed

Possible era buckets remain flexible and can be defined by coaching, roster cores, contention windows, or major transactions.

Salary, Cap & Roster-Building History

Team buildingValue and allocationPyramids · stacks · timelines

Use financial data to explain how Chicago allocated opportunity and risk. Compare salaries as a share of the team or league cap whenever possible; raw dollars across decades are rarely meaningful.

Prompt starters
  • Largest Bulls cap shares by season
  • Best-value Bulls contracts
  • How payroll concentration changed across eras
  • What Chicago spent on guards, wings, and bigs
  • Contracts that shaped an era
  • Production versus cap share

Salary history can be its own post lane or an explanatory layer inside player and era comparisons.

Historical Rankings, Records & Rarity

Historical companyRecords and outliersLeaderboards · distributions

Answer “where does this rank?” without requiring every post to be current. Career totals, single-season peaks, unusual stat combinations, and franchise milestones all fit the broader reference-board grammar.

Prompt starters
  • Top Bulls scoring stretches
  • Most complete statistical seasons
  • Youngest and oldest franchise leaders
  • Rarest Bulls stat lines
  • Playoff performances in historical company
  • Records that survived longer than expected

Skills, Impact, Roles & Lineups

Comparative boardsComponents visibleHeadshot rows · tables

Use transparent component measures to show who created, finished, rebounded, spaced the floor, or connected the offense. The same grammar can compare individuals, five-man groups, rosters, or eras.

Prompt starters
  • All-time Bulls skill leaders
  • Best playmakers by era
  • Most impactful Bulls seasons
  • Best and most-used five-man lineups
  • Which rosters had the clearest role balance?
  • Who created versus who finished?

A composite is optional. When used, its inputs and weighting should remain visible enough to debate.

Spatial & Playing-Style History

Shot geographyStyle evolutionCourts · profiles · comparisons

Use the court only when location is the question. Historical coverage may be narrower than box-score history, but the available years can still reveal changing zones, role shifts, and era fingerprints.

Prompt starters
  • Who led Chicago from each zone?
  • How a player's shot map changed over his Bulls tenure
  • Zone leaders by era
  • How Chicago moved from midrange toward the rim and three
  • Which Bulls had the most unusual spatial profiles?
  • Current player versus historical Bulls analogue

Current-to-Historical Bridges

In-season and offseasonTimely + evergreenAny suitable grammar

Connect present Bulls questions to franchise memory. During the season, a current performance supplies the hook; during the offseason, the historical comparison can stand on its own.

Prompt starters
  • Where this current stretch ranks
  • The historical Bull most similar to each current player
  • How this roster's age and salary shape compare with past teams
  • What the current lineup shares with successful Bulls units
  • Which era would use this player best?
  • What changed since the previous Bulls core?

Trust makes the archive useful

Data & Fairness Guardrails

Name the coverage window

Say “since 2000” or “shot-location era” when the source does not cover full franchise history.

Adjust when eras distort the comparison

Use pace, league efficiency, role, or opportunity context when raw totals would reward the environment more than the player.

Normalize financial comparisons

Prefer salary-cap share, payroll share, or same-season context over nominal dollars across decades.

Show the qualification rule

Keep minimum games, minutes, possessions, attempts, or seasons visible on the graphic.

Expose important metric components

A named composite can help, but readers should see the inputs and avoid mistaking editorial weighting for objective truth.

Keep sources and dates attached

Historical posts should remain auditable after the season changes and the graphic recirculates.

Process ideas waiting for their moment

Parked Threads

Workflow and tooling ideas deliberately parked — not rejected — each with the condition that wakes it up. Guiding rule while the account is young: every hour of engineering must lose the argument against “make one more good post.”

Scheduled Idea Generation

A weekly scheduled session renders 2–3 candidate mocks into the idea catalog for reaction. Parked because offseason data is frozen and forced ideas would kill the habit. Wake: October 2026, when games resume.

In-Chat Reaction Rounds

An interactive picker rendered in chat during “give me N options” rounds; the catalog file stays the durable record, chat stays the interaction layer. Wake: any options round, on request.

Catalog Interactivity

Client-side-only upgrades — filter chips by status/grammar, a lightbox for full-size viewing. No server, no stored state. Wake: when the catalog passes ~20 cards.

Auto-Posting

Rejected for now: personal accounts have no sanctioned posting API, browser automation risks the account, and taste is the product. Wake: only if the account converts to Creator — and then semi-auto (prepared post + reminder), never fully automatic.
