Postgame Experiment — Game Deciders

Mocked Comparison Postgame 2026-07-13

Reduce a game to the four margins that most often decide it: effective field-goal percentage, turnover rate, offensive-rebound rate, and free-throw scoring rate, with the Bulls and opponent on one shared comparison board.

Experiment: the most immediately understandable and repeatable of four regular-season recap directions. This mock uses the Jul 10 Bulls–Grizzlies Summer League game only as rehearsal data; a live version would use the completed regular-season game.

Open question: whether the free-throw factor should remain FTM/FGA, which reflects points converted, or switch to FTA/FGA, which better isolates foul-drawing process. No recurring format has been approved.

Shared prototype: prototypes/regular_season_gamebook.py

Postgame Experiment — Game Fingerprint

Mocked Profile Postgame 2026-07-13

Show what made one Bulls game unusual by locating its shotmaking, shot mix, free-throw rate, turnovers, and assisted-field-goal rate inside the team’s own season distribution.

Experiment: the strongest “I did not know that” direction because it turns a box-score number into team-specific context. The mock deliberately crosses Summer League game values with the 2025–26 regular-season Bulls baseline to rehearse the visual, not to make a real basketball comparison.

Open question: use the Bulls’ season-to-date distribution before the game for the cleanest baseline, then feature only the one or two genuinely unusual traits instead of forcing the same five every night.

Postgame Experiment — Shot Quality vs. Making

Mocked Bar Chart Postgame 2026-07-13

Separate shot diet from shot conversion by comparing actual field-goal points with the points an average NBA team would expect from the same basic shot-zone mix.

Experiment: the most analytically distinctive direction, best used when shot location explains the game. This rehearsal model uses 2025–26 league-average field-goal percentage by five basic zones; it is intentionally simpler than a true expected-shot model.

Limitation: location alone does not account for shooter, defender distance, clock, pass quality, or self-created difficulty. Treat it as average location value, not a complete measure of shot quality, and use it selectively rather than automatically.

Shared prototype: prototypes/regular_season_gamebook.py

Postgame Experiment — Who Drove What?

Mocked Contribution Board Postgame 2026-07-13

Map the rotation by role rather than one catch-all rating: each qualifying player’s share of team scoring, shot attempts, assists, and rebounds shows who carried which part of the game.

Experiment: the strongest Basketball University-style roster board of the set and a possible alternative to automatically making individual player slides. The rehearsal includes players who logged at least 10 minutes in Bulls–Grizzlies.

Open question: whether this belongs inside a recap carousel or works better as a standalone post when the roles split in an interesting way. It deliberately avoids leaning on noisy single-game plus-minus.

Shared prototype: prototypes/regular_season_gamebook.py
