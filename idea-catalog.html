2025–26 Bulls On-Court Performance Landscape

Posted Landscape Season Recap 2026-07-22

Map how the Bulls performed offensively and defensively during each qualifying player’s Chicago minutes, using NBA.com team ratings rather than a predictive or isolated-impact metric.

Validated direction: a 500-Bulls-minute cutoff produces 14 players and a natural break to the next player at 447.2 minutes. The revised draft is a chart-only asset using the approved Sticky Stats grammar: warm rounded panel, Helvetica, heavy left/bottom axes, metric-only axis titles, faint grid, plain two-line quadrant keys without legend dots, unoutlined player and team-reference labels, equal-size square-crop cutout headshots, and Bulls team crosshairs (112.1 OffRtg, 117.4 DefRtg). Postgame Experiment — Shot Quality vs. Making

Mocked Bar Chart Postgame 2026-07-13

Separate shot diet from shot conversion by comparing actual field-goal points with the points an average NBA team would expect from the same basic shot-zone mix.

Experiment: the most analytically distinctive direction, best used when shot location explains the game. This rehearsal model uses 2025–26 league-average field-goal percentage by five basic zones; it is intentionally simpler than a true expected-shot model.

Limitation: location alone does not account for shooter, defender distance, clock, pass quality, or self-created difficulty. Treat it as average location value, not a complete measure of shot quality, and use it selectively rather than automatically.

Shared prototype: prototypes/regular_season_gamebook.py
